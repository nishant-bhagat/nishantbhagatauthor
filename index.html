Author

Nishant Bhagat

Crime fiction where the past refuses to stay buried.

Nishant Bhagat is an author of mystery and crime fiction. His debut novel, The Darjeeling Murders, follows four friends who travel to the misty hills of Darjeeling to document a thirty-year-old killing spree, only to find that the convicted serial killer still denies the most notorious murder of them all. It is a story about memory, obsession, and the secrets small towns keep. His second mystery novel, No Such Guest, is a slow-burn closed-circle mystery set during a cyclone-struck destination wedding in the Andaman Islands, where a young woman is found dead on no guest list, no hotel register, and no ferry manifest.

The Second Novel

No Such Guest

No Such Guest is the second mystery novel by Nishant Bhagat. On the morning after a destination-wedding sangeet on Havelock Island in the Andamans, a young woman is found dead on the beach, arranged to look drowned on sand the sea never touched. She wears no identification. Half the wedding remembers her face. Not one guest can give her a name, and she appears on no guest list, no hotel register, and no ferry manifest. As a cyclone seals the island, Ira Venkataraman, the groom's cousin and a former Kolkata Police homicide detective, is trapped with a resort full of witnesses and a victim who does not officially exist. A slow-burn, closed-circle whodunit in the tradition of Agatha Christie and Keigo Higashino.

About

About the author

Nishant Bhagat writes atmospheric, character-driven mystery and crime fiction about ordinary people who go looking for the truth and find more than they bargained for. The Darjeeling Murders is his debut novel, and No Such Guest is his second.

An electronics engineer and crime fiction novelist, Nishant spent close to sixteen years in the corporate world before writing his debut novel. He is the author of two mystery novels, The Darjeeling Murders and No Such Guest.
